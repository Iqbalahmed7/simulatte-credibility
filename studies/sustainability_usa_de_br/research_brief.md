# Research Brief — Consumer Sustainability Attitudes (USA, Germany, Brazil)

**Project:** STU_004_sustainability_usa_de_br
**Domain:** Climate attitudes, sustainable consumption, attitude-behaviour gap
**Geography:** United States, Germany, Brazil
**Ground truth:** GlobeScan Radar 2024 (n≈1,000 per country); Kantar Sustainability Sector Index 2024
**Approved by:** Morpheus (autonomous research — Stage 1 self-approval)
**Date:** 2026-04-13

---

## FACT TABLE

F01  GlobeScan Radar 2024: 66% of global consumers agree "I feel a personal responsibility to do what I can for the environment" — up 4pp from 2022.   [GlobeScan Radar 2024, Global]
F02  Germany has the highest sustainability concern index of the three countries: 78% report environmental issues as personally important, vs. 61% USA and 72% Brazil.   [GlobeScan Radar 2024, Germany/USA/Brazil]
F03  Brazil shows high environmental concern (F02) but the lowest stated willingness to pay a premium: only 28% of Brazilian consumers say they "regularly pay more for sustainable products" vs. 41% Germany and 34% USA.   [GlobeScan Radar 2024, Brazil]
F04  USA consumers show the largest documented attitude-behaviour gap: 61% state environmental responsibility is personally important (F02) but only 34% report acting on it through purchase behaviour (F03).   [GlobeScan Radar 2024, USA]
F05  Greenwashing scepticism is rising across all three markets: 57% of USA, 63% of German, and 49% of Brazilian consumers say they do not trust environmental claims made by companies.   [Kantar Sustainability Sector Index 2024]
F06  Third-party certification significantly increases purchase intent: Kantar data shows a 22pp increase in intent when a recognised eco-label is present vs. brand-only claim across all three markets.   [Kantar Sustainability Sector Index 2024]
F07  Younger consumers (18–34) show higher sustainability concern than older consumers in all three markets — but similar or lower premium willingness, suggesting the gap is age-neutral on behaviour.   [GlobeScan Radar 2024]
F08  The WorldviewAnchor SCP (Social Change Pace) and ESP (Economic Security Priority) dimensions are specifically designed to model the tension between stated values and economic constraints.   [Simulatte Persona Generator architecture]
F09  Germany's "sacrificial willingness" index — willingness to accept personal inconvenience for environmental benefit — is 49%, the highest of the three markets.   [GlobeScan Radar 2024, Germany]
F10  Brazil's sustainability attitudes are strongly shaped by deforestation and biodiversity narratives, with Amazon framing generating 18pp higher concern than generic climate change framing.   [GlobeScan Radar 2024, Brazil]

G01  Whether the Persona Generator reproduces the attitude-behaviour gap magnitude across three different countries without explicit calibration to sustainability-specific data.
G02  Whether the German premium-willingness advantage over USA and Brazil (F03) emerges from country-calibrated personas from demographic and WorldviewAnchor inputs alone.
G03  Whether greenwashing scepticism distributions (F05) are reproduced accurately across the three markets.
G04  Whether the certification premium effect (F06) is reproduced across personas with different economic security priority (ESP) values.

---

## Study Objective

Test whether the Persona Generator reproduces documented sustainability attitude distributions — including the attitude-behaviour gap — across three geographically and culturally distinct markets without being calibrated to sustainability-specific training data. This is the first Simulatte study in the consumer strategy domain (vs. political attitudes), demonstrating domain generalisability. DA ≥ 91% in this domain would substantially expand the Persona Generator's commercial credibility to CMO and brand strategy buyers.
