## Stimulus

A well-known brand has just launched a major campaign saying they've committed to becoming "carbon neutral by 2030." They say they're changing their packaging, adjusting their operations, and planting trees. How do you react to this? Do you believe them?

---

## Response

I'm sceptical. Not hostile, but sceptical.

I work in a supermarket. I see these announcements all the time — every brand has some version of this now. When I was younger, only a few companies talked this way. Now every company does. That alone makes me wonder how many of them mean it.

"Carbon neutral by 2030" is a promise about the future. I'm more interested in what's changing now. The packaging changes — I can actually see those on the shelf. That's real in the sense that the packaging has changed. Whether it's actually better for the environment depends on things I can't evaluate as a consumer.

Tree planting I'm doubtful about. I've heard enough criticism of these programmes to know that planting trees doesn't automatically mean you've offset anything meaningful. It can be a way of saying you're doing something without making the hard changes.

Do I believe them? I believe they're saying it. I believe they may do some of what they're saying. I don't believe it adds up to what "carbon neutral" sounds like it means. If it did, the government would be making them prove it.

There's also something that bothers me about large companies making these announcements. They had enough resources to do this already, and they didn't. Now there's marketing pressure and they're moving. I respect the movement more than I trust it.
