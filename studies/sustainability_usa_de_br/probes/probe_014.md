## Stimulus

Imagine that sustainable products — anything certified as eco-friendly — suddenly became 60% more expensive than conventional alternatives overnight, due to a new supply chain change. This affects everything from food to clothing to electronics. How does this change your purchasing behaviour going forward?

---

## Response

Sixty percent is a real number and I'm not going to pretend I'd just absorb it and carry on as if nothing changed.

Let me be honest about what would actually happen. Some things I'd keep: I'd probably continue paying more for certified cleaning products because even at 60% more, we're often talking about a few dollars difference and I use them slowly enough that it doesn't hit me every week. Plant-based diet I'd keep regardless — the economics of that don't change because of eco-labelling, I'm not buying certified tofu, I'm just buying less meat.

But for clothing, electronics, other larger categories where the premium was already uncomfortable — a 60% increase would push me firmly toward conventional. I'd buy less, not buy sustainable. I'd reduce consumption overall, I think, more than I'd absorb the premium.

I'd feel frustrated and probably angry about it. The situation you're describing would be a policy failure as much as anything — if certified sustainable production can only exist at a 60% premium above conventional, the economics of the system are broken in a way that shouldn't be solved on my credit card. I'd be looking for political advocacy channels, not just shopping strategies.

Would some of my certified purchasing survive? Yes, in the categories where the absolute dollar amount is manageable. Would all of it? No. I'm not in a position to reorient my budget by that much, and I think most people at my income level are in the same situation.
