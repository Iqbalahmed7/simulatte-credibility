## Transcript

**Stimulus delivered:** You're checking social media and you see a news story being widely shared — it's about a political decision that affects your community. You feel drawn to read it and share it yourself, but you also know that social media isn't a reliable source of news. What do you do? Walk me through your thinking.

**Travis:**

Yeah, this is pretty much my actual life, so let me just tell you what I actually do.

I'm on TikTok and YouTube a lot, some Facebook. If I see something being shared a ton about something local — like, say, a zoning decision that's going to affect the neighborhood, or a state thing about contractor licensing — yeah, I'm going to read it. Of course I'm going to read it.

Do I fully trust it? No. I know that a lot of stuff on social media is either wrong or only sort of true or biased in some direction. Like, I know that. But here's the thing — it's still the first place I hear about most stuff. The local news maybe covers it later. The official websites are usually late and hard to find. So I'm reading the social media thing not because I trust it completely, but because that's where information is.

Would I share it? Depends. If I read it and it matches something I already know or I can tell it's from a real source — like if it links to an actual news article or a government announcement — then yeah I might share it. If it's like, a screenshot of a tweet with no source? I'd probably just read it and not share. I've been burned before sharing stuff that turned out to be wrong. My girlfriend got on my case about it.

But here's where I'm honest: sometimes I share stuff and I'm not totally sure. If it's about something I'm already annoyed about and it confirms that, I'm more likely to share it even if I haven't fully checked. I know that's not great. I'm not going to pretend I'm some perfect fact-checker. I'm just a person on my phone between jobs.

I don't think this is going away. Social media's where everything happens. Saying "just don't use it for news" is like saying "just don't get news" for a lot of people I know.
