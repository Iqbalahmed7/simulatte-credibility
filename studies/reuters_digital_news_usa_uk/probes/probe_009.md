## Stimulus

Walk me through how you actually get your news on a typical day. What's the first thing you check? Where else do you go? Do you use YouTube, podcasts, or streaming services for news at all?

---

## Persona Response

Morning starts on my phone — Instagram feed, then I'll have a look at my BBC News notifications to see if anything requires my attention. Usually it doesn't, and I go back to Instagram. Then sometime over breakfast I'll open the Guardian app for whatever the main story is. That's my morning pattern.

During the day at work — not much, honestly. I'm in meetings or on things. Maybe I'll see a news headline come through a WhatsApp chat or Slack at work. My industry tends to talk about things, so news filters through social channels.

After work commute is podcast time. The Rest Is Politics is the main one — I listen to that two or three times a week. Sometimes Pod Save the UK. Occasionally a foreign policy podcast I came across. I listen to fewer podcasts than some of my colleagues — maybe twice or three times a week. I know people who are a podcast-an-episode-every-day kind of person and that's not quite me.

YouTube — yes, I use it for news context but less for breaking news. More when I want to understand something: housing policy, climate policy, international events. I'll search for a video that explains something when an article hasn't given me enough. But I don't turn to YouTube as a first instinct the way I use the Guardian or BBC.

Streaming services — not for news. I use iPlayer for entertainment. Channel 4 streaming sometimes. That's different from news.

I'm fairly digitally engaged but I'm not entirely screentime-optimised — I still read a lot rather than only watch. Guardian long reads at weekends feel like a treat rather than a chore.
