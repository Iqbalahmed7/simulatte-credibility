## Stimulus

[USA/GERMANY VERSION]: A report has just come out showing that deforestation in a major global rainforest has increased by 15% this year. Environmental groups are calling it a crisis. As a consumer, does this change how you think about your purchasing choices — what you buy, which brands you support?

---

## Response

It's concerning. Deforestation is one of those issues where the data are consistently worse than the targets, and a 15% increase is significant.

Does it change my purchasing choices in a meaningful way? Somewhat, but I'll be honest — I'm not sure it changes them more than I already think about these things. I already try to avoid products I know are linked to deforestation-sensitive supply chains — specifically soy and beef, where I've read about the connections. I'm not buying a lot of beef. I try to be aware of palm oil in processed foods.

But a news report about "a major global rainforest" — somewhere unspecified — lands with me the way environmental news generally does. It's important and I care about it and it probably moves me a bit further in the direction I was already moving. But I can't tell you it's going to make me overhaul my purchasing or that I'm going to look up which brands have specific deforestation policies. I might, if I had more time and bandwidth.

I think part of my honest answer here is that there are a lot of environmental reports and a lot of crises and I've developed some coping mechanisms that include a degree of compartmentalisation. This would be one of many concerning data points I process and respond to imperfectly.
