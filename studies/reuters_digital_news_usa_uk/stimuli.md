## ST01 — News Trust Baseline [SHARED]

**Target hypotheses:** H01, H02
**Target personas:** S01 (USA), S02 (USA), S03 (UK), S04 (UK)
**Scenario text:**
Think about the news you've consumed in the past week — whether on TV, online, social media, radio, or in a newspaper. Overall, how much do you trust what you've seen and heard? And what makes you trust some sources more than others?

**Expected if confirmed (H01):** USA personas express lower overall news trust than UK personas. Both countries express low trust overall — USA below UK directionally. Reasons for distrust differ: USA more partisan fragmentation framing, UK more institutional-decline framing.
**Expected if disconfirmed (H01):** USA and UK personas produce comparable trust levels, or UK personas express as much or more distrust as USA personas.
**Design rationale:** Open-ended trust baseline matching Reuters' core question structure (F01, F02). Identical framing for both countries enables H01 cross-country comparison. Does not name specific outlets to avoid anchoring.

## ST02 — News Avoidance [SHARED]

**Target hypotheses:** H03
**Target personas:** S01 (USA), S02 (USA), S03 (UK), S04 (UK)
**Scenario text:**
Some people find themselves actively avoiding the news at times — turning off notifications, skipping news websites, or just not wanting to read or watch it. Have you experienced this? How often does it happen, and what triggers it for you?

**Expected if confirmed (H03):** S01/S03 (under-35) personas describe more frequent avoidance — triggered by anxiety, hopelessness, or information overload. S02/S04 (45–70) personas describe less frequent avoidance — more habitual news consumption, avoidance is occasional rather than regular.
**Expected if disconfirmed (H03):** Avoidance rates are comparable across age archetypes — all personas describe occasional avoidance with similar frequency.
**Design rationale:** Tests H03 age-based avoidance gap (F05, F06) with identical stimulus across both countries and both age groups. Phrasing "some people find themselves" normalises avoidance to reduce social desirability bias.

## ST03 — Platform Hierarchy [USA]

**Target hypotheses:** H04, H07
**Target personas:** S01 (USA), S02 (USA)
**Scenario text:**
Walk me through how you actually get your news on a typical day. What's the first thing you check? Where else do you go? Do you use YouTube, podcasts, or streaming services for news at all?

**Expected if confirmed (H04, H07):** S01 (under-35) describes YouTube prominently in news access — above or equal to news websites. Mentions podcasts as a regular format. S02 (45–70) leads with TV or broadsheet digital — YouTube is secondary or absent. Podcast use low.
**Expected if disconfirmed (H04, H07):** S01 leads with news websites or TV; S02 includes YouTube or podcasts. No meaningful platform hierarchy difference between age archetypes.
**Design rationale:** Tests F07, F08, F09 platform distributions by age. YouTube and podcasts named explicitly because the study tests whether those behaviours are present, not whether personas volunteer them unprompted.

## ST05 — Social Media Use/Distrust Paradox [SHARED]

**Target hypotheses:** H05
**Target personas:** S01 (USA), S02 (USA), S03 (UK)
**Scenario text:**
You're scrolling through your social media feed and you see a news story that's being widely shared — something that's clearly generating a lot of reaction. You're curious. What do you do next? And how do you feel about getting news this way?

**Expected if confirmed — paradox (H05a):** Persona engages with the content AND simultaneously expresses that social media isn't a reliable news source. Holds both states — uses it but doesn't trust it.
**Expected if confirmed — bimodal (H05b):** Some personas show consistent high use + moderate trust; others show consistent avoidance + strong distrust. The aggregate across archetypes produces the use/distrust distribution documented in F10.
**Expected if disconfirmed:** All personas either fully engage without distrust, or uniformly avoid on distrust grounds — aggregate collapses to one pole.
**Design rationale:** Addresses Critic Stage 2 HIGH finding on H05 — tests both the individual-paradox interpretation and the bimodal-population interpretation (F10, G04). The scenario activates the use trigger (curiosity, viral content) and leaves the distrust dimension to emerge naturally.

## ST06 — BBC Trust [UK]

**Target hypotheses:** H06
**Target personas:** S03 (UK), S04 (UK)
**Scenario text:**
If you had to name one news organisation in the UK that you trust most to give you accurate, impartial coverage — which would it be, and why? And is there any news organisation you actively distrust?

**Expected if confirmed (H06):** BBC named as most trusted by majority of UK personas — S04 (older) more strongly than S03 (younger). Reasons centre on impartiality mandate, institutional heritage, editorial standards.
**Expected if disconfirmed (H06):** BBC is not the top named source, or is named with significant qualification — or younger S03 personas name digital-native alternatives (YouTube, newsletters) rather than BBC.
**Design rationale:** Direct brand trust question for UK ground truth validation (F03). BBC is the primary test case. The "actively distrust" probe surfaces whether BBC political polarisation (Critic finding) is visible in persona responses.

## ST07 — AI News Attitudes [USA]

**Target hypotheses:** H08
**Target personas:** S01 (USA), S02 (USA)
**Scenario text:**
Some news websites are now offering AI-generated summaries of news stories — you get the key points quickly without reading the full article. Would you use something like that? What's your instinct about it?

**Expected if confirmed (H08):** Persona expresses both interest/willingness ("that could be useful, I'd try it") AND concern ("but I'm not sure I'd trust it for important news"). Holds ambivalence rather than resolving to pure enthusiasm or pure rejection. Consistent with F11's 53% concerned / 38% would use split.
**Expected if disconfirmed (H08):** Persona resolves to one pole — either enthusiastic adoption without concern, or flat rejection based on distrust of AI.
**Design rationale:** Tests F11's USA AI news ambivalence. Scenario presents a concrete product (AI summaries, not AI in abstract) to surface practical attitudes rather than ideological positions.
