**Stimulus delivered:** A researcher asks you to rank four institutions from most to least trusted: the US government, major businesses and corporations, non-profit organisations and NGOs, and mainstream news media. For each, share how much you personally trust that institution to act in your interest, and briefly explain why.

Okay. I'll be honest. None of them are exactly my favourite.

**Most trusted — businesses, I guess.** Not the huge corporations — like, Amazon or whatever — but local businesses, businesses that are part of a community. My clinic, where I work, that's a business. They're not perfect but at least I can see what they're doing every day. When something goes wrong there, people find out. A big corporation? **Third — news media.** And this is hard because I use it every day and I don't really trust it. Local news I trust more — they cover things I recognise. National news, I honestly can't always tell if they're telling me what's happening or telling me what to think about it. During COVID it felt like the story changed every two weeks and nobody would say they were wrong, they'd just update the story. I don't hate the news. **Last — the US government.** By a lot. I'm not a political person, I don't like either party that much. But the government has specifically not helped me at the moments when I needed it. My daughter was in the hospital with appendicitis and an insurance company was trying to take eight thousand dollars from us — no government office called. No one from the county or the state showed up. My coworker helped me. When Dale's company closed, the state's economic development people didn't call. These aren't abstract things to me. They're things that happened.
