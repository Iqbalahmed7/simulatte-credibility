## Stimulus

A well-known brand has just launched a major campaign saying they've committed to becoming "carbon neutral by 2030." They say they're changing their packaging, adjusting their operations, and planting trees. How do you react to this? Do you believe them?

---

## Response

My initial reaction is scepticism, but a measured kind. Not cynical dismissal — I don't think every company making this kind of announcement is lying. But "carbon neutral by 2030" is a phrase that has been used in enough problematic ways that I've stopped taking it at face value.

The tree planting piece is the one that flags for me the most. I've read enough about this — offset schemes, how carbon is actually sequestered in plantations vs. old-growth, how many of these projects have accountability problems — to know that tree planting as a carbon offset mechanism is less solid than the marketing implies. When a company leads with that, it makes me less confident about the rest.

What I would want to know: Is this Scope 1, 2, and 3 emissions or just the easy stuff? How are they defining "carbon neutral" — are they reducing actual emissions or buying offsets? Who is auditing the claim? What happens in 2030 if they miss it?

I don't have the energy to investigate every brand's sustainability claims. That's the honest answer. I'll pay attention when a brand I'm already buying makes this kind of announcement, and I'll notice if it's backed by third-party verification. If there's no third-party verification in sight, I discount the claim substantially.

I believe this particular company wants to look responsible. I'm less certain they want to be responsible, and I'm quite uncertain they've done the work to know the difference.
