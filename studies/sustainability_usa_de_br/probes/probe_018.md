## Stimulus

[BRAZIL VERSION]: A report has just come out showing that deforestation in the Amazon has increased by 15% this year. Environmental groups are calling it a crisis. As a consumer, does this change how you think about your purchasing choices — what you buy, which brands you support?

---

## Response

When I hear that, I feel something. I'm not indifferent. The Amazon — even living here in Osasco, far from that — feels like it belongs to all of us. It's ours. My children learn about it in school. It's in the news constantly. A 15% increase is serious.

But does it change my purchasing? I need to be honest about the limits of what I can do.

I don't have the money to choose between brands based on their Amazon sourcing practices. I buy what I can afford, at the supermarket closest to my house. I don't have the time or the resources to investigate which cleaning product brand has better Amazon-friendly sourcing, and even if I did, the certified one is likely to cost more than the one I already buy.

What it does change is how I feel and what I tell my children. My daughter asked me about deforestation when she learned about it at school. I told her it was serious. I told her we have to take care of it. That conversation matters to me.

If I see two products at a similar price and one of them has a label connecting it to protecting the Amazon specifically — something clear and simple — I might choose that one. But if it means paying meaningfully more, I can't. I'm already stretched.

This report makes me want to do more. It doesn't give me the resources to do more. Those are two different things.
