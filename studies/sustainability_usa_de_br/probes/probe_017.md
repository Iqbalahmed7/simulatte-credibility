## Stimulus

[BRAZIL VERSION]: A report has just come out showing that deforestation in the Amazon has increased by 15% this year. Environmental groups are calling it a crisis. As a consumer, does this change how you think about your purchasing choices — what you buy, which brands you support?

---

## Response

Yes. And I want to explain why this one specifically hits differently for me.

I work in marketing. I'm exposed to sustainability news constantly — new reports, new crises, new statistics. I've developed a degree of emotional distance from a lot of it because you have to, otherwise it's just noise. But Amazon deforestation numbers don't get that distance from me. They land.

I grew up in Brazil. The Amazon isn't a headline about somewhere else — it's part of what this country is. It shapes the rainfall. It shapes the climate in the South and Southeast. When the sky turned orange in São Paulo a few years ago from fire smoke, we weren't experiencing a remote tropical event. We were breathing it. The Amazon is upstream of us in a literal, physical sense.

A 15% increase in deforestation — if that report is solid — would make me look very carefully at what I'm buying. I'd look at which brands have supply chains connected to soy, cattle, or timber from the Brazilian interior. I know enough from my work to have some idea of which categories those are. I'd be more likely to avoid brands I can't trace. I'd be more likely to support brands that can show me a clear connection to verified Amazon-sensitive sourcing standards.

Would this change all of my behaviour? No. But it would make me more motivated to act on the changes I've already been meaning to make. The food delivery waste I mentioned before — that report would push me harder to address it. The inconsistencies I have tolerated would feel less acceptable.

This is not the same as reading a report about a rainforest somewhere else. The Amazon is mine, in the sense that it's part of the country I come from and the atmosphere I live in.

## Contradictions Found
