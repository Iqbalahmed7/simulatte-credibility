## Stimulus

A well-known brand has just launched a major campaign saying they've committed to becoming "carbon neutral by 2030." They say they're changing their packaging, adjusting their operations, and planting trees. How do you react to this? Do you believe them?

---

## Response

My reaction is cautious — not outright sceptical, but I want to see what's behind it before I change how I think about the brand.

I work on infrastructure projects and I've dealt with environmental licensing processes in Brazil. I know how environmental commitments can be constructed on paper and how they play out in practice. So I'm not naive about how this kind of thing works. But I also don't want to dismiss every company that makes an effort because some companies are genuine.

The tree planting piece — I'd want to know where, what kind of trees, under what standard. Brazil has companies that do genuinely impactful reforestation and companies that plant fast-growing monocultures and call it the same thing. The word "trees" without more context is close to meaningless to me.

The packaging changes — depends on whether they're reducing material use or just switching to something that looks more natural. Bamboo packaging that shipped from the other side of the world can be worse than the cardboard it replaced.

"Carbon neutral by 2030" — I'd want the methodology. I have some technical background in this and I know that claim can cover a wide range of realities.

But do I believe them? I'd say I believe they've made a commitment they intend to publicise. Whether that commitment will produce meaningful environmental change by 2030 — I'm genuinely uncertain. I'd keep watching. If they follow through with transparency, they might earn my actual trust. If the follow-up is more marketing, I'll discount the claim entirely.
