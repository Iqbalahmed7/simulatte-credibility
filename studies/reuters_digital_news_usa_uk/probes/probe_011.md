## Stimulus

You're scrolling through your social media feed and you see a news story that's being widely shared — something that's clearly generating a lot of reaction. You're curious. What do you do next? And how do you feel about getting news this way?

---

## Persona Response

Okay, this is very much my life. I'm going to be honest about the whole sequence because I think it's more complicated than most people admit.

I see the story. I feel curious — probably before I've read a single word, just from the thumbnail and the number of reactions. I tap it. I read the headline and the lede. Then I do something I've trained myself to do: I check who posted it, whether the outlet is one I know, and whether the emotional charge of the headline matches what's likely to be in the story. This takes about eight seconds. Then I decide whether to keep reading or not.

Most of the time: I keep reading. And while I'm reading, I'm holding two things simultaneously. One part of my brain is processing the information. Another part is running a background process of 'but how was this chosen to go viral? What is this story doing to my emotional state? What is missing from this framing?' I do this constantly. It's exhausting, but I can't turn it off.

Do I trust social media as a news source? No. Genuinely, no. I don't think the aggregation of what gets amplified on social media reflects a coherent editorial judgment about what's important. I think it reflects what provokes a reaction. Those are not the same thing.

But do I use it for news? Yes. I use it every day. I get a meaningful percentage of my first-awareness of stories from Instagram and Twitter-adjacent spaces. There's no contradiction I can resolve there. It's just the reality of where information lives now. Refusing to engage with social media news would mean being less informed, not more — at least about certain types of stories that the mainstream news cycle picks up late or ignores.

I'm a UX researcher. I have spent professional time studying how platform design shapes behaviour. I have the conceptual vocabulary to understand exactly what's happening to me when I scroll. And I still do it. That's the most honest thing I can tell you about social media and news.
