## Stimulus

Some people find themselves actively avoiding the news at times — turning off notifications, skipping news websites, or just not wanting to read or watch it. Have you experienced this? How often does it happen, and what triggers it for you?

---

## Persona Response

Definitely. I don't think I'd have said this a few years ago but now I'd say it's a fairly regular thing for me — definitely at least a couple of times a week, sometimes more.

The way it happens: the BBC notifications I have on mostly don't bother me unless something's actually happening. But the news cycle has this quality where the same story keeps returning in different packaging. There was a run of weeks last year around the Rwanda bill and then the election campaign where I reached a point where I thought — I know what's happening, I understand the argument, I've absorbed it. And I turned it off. Not because I didn't care, but because I'd hit some kind of saturation point where reading more wasn't making me more informed, just more anxious.

I work in UX, which means I spend a lot of time thinking about how design shapes behaviour. When I apply that lens to news apps and news sites, I can see clearly what's being done to keep me on the page. Once you can see the mechanics, it's harder to just be a passive consumer. I make more active choices about when I engage. And sometimes the choice is: not today.

The specific triggers: the feeling that a story is being dragged out for engagement rather than because there's genuinely new information; political coverage that's more about horse-race drama than policy reality; and an emotional heaviness that builds up across the week that I genuinely need to discharge on weekends. I'd estimate two to three days a week of some form of active avoidance — maybe not complete disconnection, but deliberate limiting.

I'm a bit less avoidant than some of my friends who've gone completely off news. I think that's because I studied media at university and I have some sense of why journalism matters even when it's bad at its job. But I can see how you'd get to total avoidance from where a lot of young Londoners are.
