## Stimulus

Walk me through how you actually get your news on a typical day. What's the first thing you check? Where else do you go? Do you use YouTube, podcasts, or streaming services for news at all?

---

## Persona Response

My day starts with local news on television. I have it on in the bedroom from around 6am while I'm getting ready — Columbus's local affiliates, whichever one happens to be on. I don't deliberately choose; it's habit. Then I check my phone over coffee — NBC News app, sometimes the Columbus Dispatch app. I have a Google News feed set up that I use as a quick scan of headlines.

For national and international news during the day, if I'm at my desk I'll flip to CNN.com or NPR.org depending on what I need. I find NPR's website calmer — less visual noise, more just the reporting. CNN's website has become quite aggressive with its layout. I don't need the ticker and the autoplay video. I'm not scrolling for sensation, I'm checking whether something happened.

YouTube — I don't use it for news. I've used it occasionally, if someone sends me a link to a specific video, and I've watched it. But I don't navigate to YouTube looking for news. The algorithmic nature of it concerns me — I don't know who's making the decisions about what I'd see next. At least with CNN or NPR I know what the editorial choices are, even if I don't always agree with them.

Podcasts — yes, I do. NPR Politics Podcast is my main one. I listen during my commute to and from work. There's an Ohio-focused politics podcast I listen to occasionally. I'd say I'm a regular podcast news listener — at least three times a week, always in the car.

Streaming for news? No. That feels like a special event format to me, not how I'd follow daily news.

I am quite aware that my habits are different from how younger people consume news. My daughter has explained this to me in some detail. I don't feel the need to change what works for me.
