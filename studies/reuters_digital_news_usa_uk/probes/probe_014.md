## Stimulus

If you had to name one news organisation in the UK that you trust most to give you accurate, impartial coverage — which would it be, and why? And is there any news organisation you actively distrust?

---

## Persona Response

The BBC. Without question, though that answer requires some careful qualification which I'll come to.

The BBC is the most important news organisation in the United Kingdom and has been for the better part of a century. Its founding mission — to inform, educate and entertain, with an obligation of impartiality that is written into its charter — is the closest thing British broadcasting has to a constitutional principle. Whether it always meets that standard is a different question from whether the standard itself is valuable and whether the BBC is the institutional vehicle best positioned to uphold it. I believe both to be true.

My qualification is this: I have watched the BBC become considerably more cautious in its editorial judgements since the Hutton Inquiry in 2003 and 2004. The inquiry resulted in a BBC that was institutionally humiliated and an organisation that drew the lesson — in my view wrongly — that challenge to government should be more carefully managed. The courage of Andrew Gilligan's original reporting on the dossier, whatever its flaws in rigour, represented a journalism of consequence. I am not sure the BBC would commission that kind of journalism now. What it has substituted is a very careful form of political balance that sometimes tips into false equivalence — giving equal weight to claims that are not equally supported by evidence. That is a failure mode of a different kind.

But is it the most trustworthy? Yes. The alternatives I find genuinely unacceptable. I will not name specific outlets as actively distrusted because as a former solicitor I am careful about that kind of characterisation. I will say that I consider several national newspapers to be engaged in the production of politically motivated content that would not survive a rigorous fact-checking process, and I consume them with corresponding scepticism if I consume them at all.
