What was measured

The methodology

A synthetic US general population of 60 personas was asked 15 Pew Research Center survey questions covering economy, policy, values, and trust. Distribution accuracy is computed as: 1 − Σ|real_i − sim_i| / 2 — a score of 100% means the simulation perfectly mirrors the real human distribution. The theoretical maximum is 91.0%, the human self-consistency ceiling measured at Stanford.

Sprint B-10 deep dive

The option-vocabulary pattern

Sprint B-10 identified and solved a systematic failure mode: abstract stance descriptions cause the survey model to default to the modal center option, regardless of how strong the language is. The fix is to mirror exact survey option text inside the persona stance — anchoring the model's choice at the vocabulary level.

Problem — Sprint B-9 (abstract)

Abstract descriptions like "your media trust is very low" or "you strongly distrust mainstream news" don't anchor the model to any specific survey option.

Result: Haiku defaulted all conservative, moderate, and progressive personas to option B ("Some") — collapsing the distribution to 75% B. q13 accuracy: 63.7%.

Fix — Sprint B-10 (option-vocabulary)

Stance language now uses the exact wording of survey options, with explicit negation of adjacent options to prevent drift.

Conservative: "none at all — not some, not not much, but none"
Lean-cons: "not much — not none at all, but not much"
Progressive: "a lot — not just some, but a lot"

Result: q13 accuracy jumped from 63.7% → 80.5%. +15.1 pp. A responses recovered to 13.3% (vs Pew 10.1%).

Under the hood

CoreMemory architecture

Each persona is built around a CoreMemory structure — a set of dedicated, separately-injected fields that prevent stance cross-contamination across question types. The key insight from Sprint B-1 onward: mixing stances into a single free-text field causes the survey model to blend them inappropriately.

Political era isolation

Current conditions stance (right track / wrong track) lives in its own current_conditions_stance field — injected as a labeled line in the system prompt, not mixed with values. Prevents economic sentiment from contaminating values-based questions.

Media trust stance field

Dedicated media_trust_stance field added in B-9, escalated in B-10 with option-vocabulary anchors. Labeled injection: "Your relationship with national news media: …"

Per-lean policy stances

Six political leans × topic-specific stance dictionaries covering guns, climate, abortion, immigration, racial equality, healthcare, democracy, and AI. Stance selection is deterministic at persona-generation time.

Option-vocabulary anchoring

Stances now mirror exact survey option wording with explicit negation of adjacent options: "your answer is none at all — not some, not not much, but none." Forces the survey model to map stance → specific option rather than re-interpreting abstract descriptions.
