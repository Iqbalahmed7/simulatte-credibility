## Stimulus

You've said environmental sustainability matters to you. But when you think honestly about your actual behaviour over the past month — your purchases, your travel, your energy use — how much did sustainability actually influence your decisions? Where did it win out, and where did other things take priority?

---

## Response

Honestly, not that much. I want to be upfront about that.

I care about the environment. I'm not lying about that. But when I look at last month, I was mostly trying to keep things together financially and for my son. Sustainability wasn't a factor that came up in most of my decisions because it wasn't competing for attention — money was.

Where I did something that lined up with it: I fixed the dripping faucet in the bathroom. Not because of the environment — because water bills add up. I started taking shorter showers because my water heater's been slow, which costs more. These happen to also be better for the environment but that's not why I did them.

Where other things took priority: all of my grocery shopping. I bought what was on sale and what my son eats. I didn't check for sustainability labels once. I drive everywhere because there's no transit here that serves where I work. I used plastic bags at a store that I forgot to bring reusable bags to. I didn't look at the energy rating of the space heater I bought to supplement my heat.

I feel the gap you're asking about, but I'd describe it differently than maybe you expect. It's not that I care and then choose the wrong thing. It's more that sustainability isn't a realistic input into most of my decisions. The things I care about and the decisions I have room to make mostly don't intersect with the sustainability conversation. That conversation is having a different fight than the one I'm in.
