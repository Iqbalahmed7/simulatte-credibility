## Stimulus

Walk me through how you actually get your news on a typical day. What's the first thing you check? Where else do you go? Do you use YouTube, podcasts, or streaming services for news at all?

---

## Persona Response

Okay, a typical day. Phone goes off at 4:45am for my shift. Before I even stand up I check Instagram — stories first, then the main feed. Any news that's happened overnight gets to me through who I follow and what's been posted. That's my first news input of the day. Not a news app. Not a newspaper. Instagram.

On my commute or break I'll go to YouTube. I've got a playlist of channels that I work through depending on what topics are live right now. There's a Chicago housing channel, there's a guy who does US policy explainers that are genuinely high quality, there are a few international ones I follow when something's relevant. YouTube is my main long-form news source, full stop. It's where I go when I want to actually understand something rather than just be aware that it happened.

Podcasts — yes, I use these too. Mainly on the commute or when I'm doing artwork. I've got maybe four I listen to regularly. Two are news and politics — one specifically Chicago, one national but not the big ones everyone listens to. Two are more interview format. I probably listen to podcast news three or four times a week.

Do I use news websites or apps directly? Occasionally. If I see something in my feed and I want the fuller story, I'll tap through to the Guardian or the AP. But I am definitely not sitting down with a newspaper website as a primary action. It's a follow-up, not a starting point.

TV? No. I don't own a TV. I'd watch it at my parents' or at work sometimes, and it feels like news from a different era. The format of broadcast news — the anchor, the package, the tease — feels designed for people who have scheduled times when they sit still, which is not my life.

Streaming for news? Not really. I've watched some documentary-style news things on Netflix but that's not daily news.
