## Stimulus

You've said environmental sustainability matters to you. But when you think honestly about your actual behaviour over the past month — your purchases, your travel, your energy use — how much did sustainability actually influence your decisions? Where did it win out, and where did other things take priority?

---

## Response

This is the question I find hardest to answer honestly, because the honest answer is uncomfortable.

Where sustainability won out in the past month: I didn't buy new clothes. I mostly stuck to my plant-based diet. I took transit to work every day — though I'd do that anyway because driving downtown Portland is miserable, so I'm not sure that fully counts. I chose the certified cleaning products when I restocked. Those are real choices.

Where other things took priority: I ordered food delivery seven times this month. Convenience beat the packaging waste every time. I know this. I've known it for months. The containers pile up before the recycling run and I feel bad about it. I have not stopped ordering delivery.

I also flew home to see my parents in November. I've already mentioned this. I bought carbon offsets, which I understand are imperfect. The visit happened. The flight happened. My concern about it was real and it produced offsets, not a decision not to go.

The food I bought in the grocery store — I didn't always check labels carefully. Tired Tuesday shopping is different from aspirational-self shopping. Some of that food came in unnecessary plastic. I noticed in the moment, thought "I should find a better option for this," and bought it anyway.

What I'm left with is that my values are clearly real — I'm not pretending to care. But they operate at a different intensity than the friction of daily life. The sustainability calculation wins when it's easy or when it's already been pre-decided at a structural level (diet, no second car, transit). It loses when the friction is immediate, the stakes feel moderate, and I'm tired.

I don't resolve this by deciding I'm a hypocrite or by deciding the gap doesn't matter. I hold it. It's the most honest description of where I am.
