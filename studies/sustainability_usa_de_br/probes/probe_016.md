## Stimulus

Imagine that sustainable products — anything certified as eco-friendly — suddenly became 60% more expensive than conventional alternatives overnight, due to a new supply chain change. This affects everything from food to clothing to electronics. How does this change your purchasing behaviour going forward?

---

## Response

That would change most of it, honestly.

I'll be transparent: right now I have a modest surplus each month. My certified cleaning products, the food choices I make around sustainability — these fit within what I can afford if I'm thoughtful. A 60% increase overnight changes that maths completely. São Paulo is expensive. My rent, my transport, my food — these eat most of what I earn.

The cleaning products I've been buying would probably be the last thing to go. The gap there in absolute reais is manageable at 60% more. But food — anything certified in food — I'd probably have to let go. That's a category where I'm already making trade-offs. A 60% premium on top of the premium I'm already paying would cross a line.

Clothing I almost never buy new anyway — I use Enjoei and vintage shops. That wouldn't change much.

What would stay: my general orientation. I'd still avoid obvious bad actors where I can identify them. I'd still try to reduce overall consumption. The behaviour that doesn't cost extra — public transit, less meat at home — those I'd keep. But the premium purchases? Most of them would go.

I feel something uncomfortable about that answer, because I believe in what these certifications represent. But I also live in Brazil, where economic reality is not abstract, and 60% overnight on top of an already elevated price point would push certified sustainable products out of my practical reach for most categories.
